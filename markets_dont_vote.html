Markets Don't Care About Your Politics

Both political investors underperform massively. Blue-Only Brenda misses half the market's gains and ends with $2.88M — $1.27M less than staying invested. Red-Only Ron does even worse at $533K — largely because he sat in cash during the Clinton boom and the Obama recovery, two of the strongest bull runs in history.

Peacetime Pat loses $1.56M by sitting out during the Afghanistan/Iraq era (2001–2014), missing the entire post-GFC recovery that drove the market from 670 to over 2,000. Wars are terrible. Markets during wartime are not.

Red-Only Ron invested slightly MORE time than Brenda (51% vs 49%) but earned dramatically less ($533K vs $2.88M). It's not about how much time you're invested — it's about which time you miss.

The lesson: Markets respond to earnings, innovation, and monetary policy — not to which party holds the White House or whether troops are deployed. Every day out of the market is a day your money isn't compounding. The investors who stayed invested through every president and every conflict came out ahead — every time.

The Advisor Conversation

When a client says "I want to move to cash because of the election" or "I'm worried about the war" — this is the data that reframes the conversation:

For the political timer: "I understand the concern. But look at the data — investors who only invested during [their preferred party] ended up with $1.3M to $3.6M less than those who stayed invested through all administrations. Markets have generated positive returns under both parties. Missing even one bull run while waiting for your party can cost decades of compounding."
For the geopolitical worrier: "I know it feels counterintuitive, but the market recovered and grew during every major conflict of the past 40 years. Sitting out during the Afghanistan/Iraq era meant missing the entire recovery from 670 to 2,000. The market prices in geopolitical risk almost immediately — by the time you read the headline, it's already in the price."
Methodology & Disclosures

Important: This analysis is hypothetical and for educational purposes only. Past performance does not guarantee future results. This is not a political statement — it is a mathematical observation about market returns during different administrations.

Data: S&P 500 Index daily closing prices, Jan 1985 – Mar 2026. Source: WSJ. Indexes cannot be invested in directly. Does not include dividends.

Presidents: Reagan (R, 1981–89), H.W. Bush (R, 1989–93), Clinton (D, 1993–01), W. Bush (R, 2001–09), Obama (D, 2009–17), Trump (R, 2017–21), Biden (D, 2021–25), Trump (R, 2025–). Transitions modeled on inauguration day.

War periods: Gulf War (Aug 1990–Feb 1991), Afghanistan (Oct 2001–Dec 2014), Iraq (Mar 2003–Dec 2011). Only major US military operations included.

Cash returns: Historical T-bill rates by year (0.1% during ZIRP to 8% in 1989).
