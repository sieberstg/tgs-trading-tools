Data Source: Index closing prices are sourced from the CRSP US Total Market Index (CRSPTM1) as published by the Wall Street Journal Historical Prices. Data begins at the index inception date of March 31, 2011 (base value = 1,000.00). Prices are updated manually and may not reflect the most recent trading session.

Methodology: The rebalance monitoring system tracks the CRSPTM1 index using a 10% drawdown threshold from the most recent local high (breakthrough). A breakthrough occurs when the index closes above its previous high, resetting the rebalance threshold to 90% of the new high. A rebalance signal is triggered when the index closes at or below the current threshold, at which point the threshold resets to 90% of the prior threshold level. Signals prior to January 2022 are theoretical back-tests illustrating how the methodology would have performed historically; the 10% rebalancing protocol was officially established in January 2022.

Rebalance Implementation: A rebalance signal on this dashboard does not mean that a portfolio rebalance was executed for any particular client. Actual rebalancing decisions depend on individual client circumstances, including but not limited to: available cash flow, rebalances that may have already occurred based on the 2.5% portfolio drift parameter, tax implications (including capital gains considerations and tax-loss harvesting opportunities), client-specific investment policy statements, account type and restrictions, and advisor discretion. Not all clients will have been rebalanced at every signal, and some clients may have been rebalanced at times that do not correspond to signals shown here.
